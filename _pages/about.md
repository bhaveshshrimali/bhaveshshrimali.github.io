I am a second year Ph.D. student in the Department of [Civil and Environmental Engineering](https://cee.illinois.edu/areas/structural-engineering) with a minor in [Computational Science and Engineering](https://cse.illinois.edu/) at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I am advised by [Prof. Oscar Lopez-Pamies](http://pamies.cee.illinois.edu/). My research focuses on understanding the macroscopic constitutive properties of composites directly in terms of their microscopic (local constitutive) properties. For this, I use a multitude of techniques ranging from analytical [Homogenization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micromechanics) to [Finite Elements](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WwgrAH-IMOk) to [Multi-Scale analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple-scale_analysis). More recently I have been trying to understand the macroscopic behavior of perforated plates. I take keen interest in pure and applied mathematics, numerical analysis and finite element methods. Following my desire to learn and explore, I have taken a plethora of courses in diverse fields ranging from core Civil Engineering classes such as Structural Steel Design, to more applied courses in [Computer Science](https://relate.cs.illinois.edu/course/cs555-s17/) and [Numerical Analysis](http://heath.cs.illinois.edu/scicomp/) such as [Multigrid Methods](https://relate.cs.illinois.edu/course/cs556-f18/), [Integral Equations and Fast Algorithms](https://relate.cs.illinois.edu/course/cs598apk-f17/), to even more abstract courses in Theoretical and Applied Mechanics such as Asymptotic Methods, Advanced Finite Elements, Partial Differential Equations and finally my research area of Constitutive Modeling and Nonlinear Finite Elements, Computational Inelasticity and Generalized Finite Element Methods.
