Bhavesh Shrimali

I am a Ph.D. student in Structural Engineering at Illinois working with Prof. Oscar Lopez-Pamies. My primary research area is theoretical and computational mechanics, where I work in the broad domain of analytical and numerical Homogenization. This involves studying PDEs whose coefficients oscillate at different scales. A natural manifestation of such PDEs occurs in highly heterogeneous materials such as composites. Currently I am working on the homogenization of porous/perforated composites. Besides research, I am also pursuing a minor in Computational Science and Engineering

Research

I'm interested in solving fundamental problems in Applied Mathematics and Numerical Analysis. A large component of my research spans around discretizing PDEs using the Finite Element Method, and more often than not using FEniCS.
